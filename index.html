Save the
Olympic National Forest

The Olympic National Forest is located on the pacific coast in Washington State. It contains one of the last rainforests in the United States, and is also known for being one of the most quiet and serene places because of its airspace restrictions and isolation.

The Problem

The Whidbey Island Naval Base is attempting to get clearance from the US National Park Service to develop parts of the Olympic National Park for training purposes.

Why should I care?

Developing parts of the Olympic National Park would threaten the biodiversity of one of the last rainforests in the Pacific Northwest. More buildings would strip the park of its natural beauty. Its serenity would become chaos.
